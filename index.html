Design in the AI PDLC. Roles & Responsibilities.

How design participates in pod work. What design owns across pods. How design's involvement flexes with the work without losing ownership of the user experience.

The Shift · From handoff to embedded
Design works inside the pod, from problem framing through built solution and beyond. Hypotheses, solutions, and the user experience are shaped together.

01Two pillars of the design role

With Pods

Design Engagement

Design joins the pod from problem framing through release, co-creating hypotheses and solutions alongside PM and engineering.
  • Joins the pod when it forms, not after
  • Helps shape hypotheses and proposed solutions with PM and engineering
  • Produces prototypes and implementation specs for engineering when applicable
  • Pairs with engineering through build
  • Design QA before shipping
Across Pods

Systems & Standards

Design owns the systems and standards Product and Engineering build on.
  • Grove and other Bloomerang design systems
  • Storybook infrastructure as the shared source of truth for the frontend
  • Accessibility standards and pattern documentation
  • Built ahead of pods so pods can move fast

02Design at each PDLC stage

Design participates from problem to release and beyond. Depth varies, not participation.

Stage 2 · Discover & Question
  • Push back on prescribed solutions
  • Shape problem statement around user experience
Stage 3 · Hypothesize & Frame
  • Push for alternative hypotheses
  • Sketch and explore proposed solutions
  • Surface design system implications
  • Spot the UX angle in work that may look purely technical
Stage 4 · Build & Validate
  • Prototype and implementation spec
  • Pair with engineering through build
  • Resolve edge cases mid-build
  • Design QA before shipping
Stage 5 · Measure & Iterate
  • Analyze user behavior signals and feedback
  • Validate against original user problem statement
  • Feed observations into future framing

04How design flexes by problem shape

Not every problem needs the same shape of design involvement. What stays constant: a designer participates in the conversation, and design judgment determines what the work needs. The shape of participation varies with the work.

01

Light design output

The work Solutions with established patterns. UI and UX implications are either absent or light: a small UI tweak following an existing pattern, a copy update with light UX considerations, work that doesn't require new design exploration.
Design's role A designer joins the problem and hypothesis conversation and confirms whether the work has UI implications, UX implications, both, or neither. If light, design weighs in on the approach, makes sure the change fits the existing experience, and returns for QA before shipping. If neither, design exits. If either surfaces along the way as more significant than expected, the work moves into Shape 2.
What it needs to stay healthy A designer in the conversation early enough to make the call. UI and UX are different lenses, and either can show up in work that didn't appear to have it at first. The determination of how much design is needed is one design makes, not an assumption the pod makes without us.
02

Design works some pieces independently, stays connected to the pod

The work A solution where design needs focused time to work through interaction patterns, prototype options, or run a UX exercise. The pod is moving in parallel on the parts that don't require design mockups or prototypes.
Design's role Embedded in problem and hypothesis with the rest of the pod. Takes the focused time needed when interaction or UX work calls for it. Comes back to the pod with options to react to, not finished answers.
What it needs to stay healthy The pod knows what design is working on and what hypothesis it's testing. Convergence is planned, not implicit. The work comes back as options, not a reveal. Design owns the final call on which design option moves forward.
03

Heavy design from the start

The work Major redesigns, net-new product surfaces, cross-platform work, or work that establishes new patterns. The user experience is the central question, not a downstream consideration.
Design's role Sustained design time on UX direction, prototypes, system contributions. Frequent, tight convergence with PM and engineering so the work shapes together as it develops.
What it needs to stay healthy Convergence is frequent, not occasional. Design brings options to the pod throughout, not just at the end. The size of the work doesn't become an excuse to split along role lines.
The shape of design's involvement flexes with the work. Design's connection to the pod doesn't.

05What success looks like

Design judgment shapes the work from problem to release and beyond.
Hypotheses, solutions, and UX shaped in the pod, not handed in.
Grove, Storybook, and our design systems give pods something solid to build on.
Patterns from pod work feed back into the design system.
